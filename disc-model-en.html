Dominance

Direct, Results-Oriented

People with a D style are direct, decisive, and results-oriented. They prefer to make quick decisions and take risks. They are motivated by challenges and competition.

In communication, they prefer short, to-the-point messages. They focus on "what" not "how". They may seem abrupt or impatient to others.

Direct Decisive Competitive Results-oriented

D Dominance

The D style focuses on results and action. D people are direct, decisive, and goal-oriented. They take control, accept challenges, and solve problems quickly. They may be perceived as demanding or impatient.

I Influence

The I style focuses on interactions and enthusiasm. I people are sociable, optimistic, and collaborative. They excel at persuasion and networking. They may be perceived as disorganized or overly optimistic.

S Steadiness

The S style focuses on cooperation and stability. S people are patient, reliable, and team-oriented. They prefer predictable environments and harmonious relationships. They may be resistant to change.

C Conscientiousness

The C style focuses on accuracy and quality. C people are analytical, systematic, and detail-oriented. They seek data and evidence before making decisions. They may be perceived as overly critical or perfectionist.
