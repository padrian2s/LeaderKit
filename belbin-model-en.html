Belbin Team Role Theory

Dr. Meredith Belbin discovered that high-performing teams need a balanced combination of different behaviors. He identified 9 distinct roles that people adopt in teams, grouped into 3 categories.

Action-Oriented

These roles transform ideas into concrete actions and ensure task completion.

  • Shaper (SH) - Sets the pace and direction
  • Implementer (IMP) - Transforms ideas into practical actions
  • Completer Finisher (CF) - Ensures quality and completion

People-Oriented

These roles facilitate collaboration, communication, and harmony within the team.

  • Coordinator (CO) - Clarifies objectives and delegates
  • Teamworker (TW) - Maintains harmony and cooperation
  • Resource Investigator (RI) - Explores and brings external resources

Thinking-Oriented

These roles bring new ideas, objective evaluation, and technical expertise.

  • Plant (PL) - Generates creative and unconventional ideas
  • Monitor Evaluator (ME) - Objectively analyzes options
  • Specialist (SP) - Brings deep technical knowledge

Team Balance

There is no "good" or "bad" role. A high-performing team needs all roles covered. People typically have 2-3 preferred roles and may also have avoided roles. Understanding these roles helps form complementary teams and reduce conflicts.
