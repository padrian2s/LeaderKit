The Big Five Model

Big Five (or OCEAN) is the most validated and accepted personality model in academic psychology. It was developed through factor analysis of thousands of descriptive terms and has been replicated across multiple cultures and languages. Unlike other models, Big Five doesn't categorize people into types, but measures degrees on each dimension.

Facets of Each Dimension

Openness

  • Active imagination
  • Aesthetic sensitivity
  • Attention to inner feelings
  • Preference for variety
  • Intellectual curiosity
  • Openness to new values

Conscientiousness

  • Competence
  • Order and organization
  • Sense of duty
  • Achievement orientation
  • Self-discipline
  • Deliberation (thinking before acting)

Extraversion

  • Warmth and friendliness
  • Sociability
  • Assertiveness
  • Activity level
  • Sensation seeking
  • Positive emotions

Agreeableness

  • Trust in others
  • Sincerity
  • Altruism
  • Compliance
  • Modesty
  • Tender-mindedness

Neuroticism (Emotional Instability)

  • Anxiety
  • Hostility / Irritability
  • Depression
  • Self-consciousness
  • Impulsivity
  • Vulnerability to stress

Differences from Other Models

Big Five is a dimensional model, not typological. It doesn't say "you are type X," but rather "you have this level on each dimension." Each person has a unique profile. Studies show that these traits are relatively stable over time (after age 30), have a significant genetic component, and predict important life outcomes (job performance, health, relationships).
